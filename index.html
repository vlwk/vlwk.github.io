Hi! I graduated from Raffles Institution in 2019 and recently got my A-Level results back, achieving straight As in all subjects offered (perfect 90 RP) with 8 distinctions including a Distinction in H3 Mathematics. While waiting to enlist into National Service in end-March, I'm currently giving some Math tuition on the side - check out my Carousell listing here ;-)

My mum signed me up for piano classes when I was only 4. My mum, just like any other “kiasu” Singaporean parent, made me practise the piano two hours a day, while I lamented as my peers got to spend the same time playing games on their iPad.

I'm blessed with perfect pitch – that is, I can recognise a note played immediately with tremendous certainty, and can sing a prceise note when asked to. When I hear a song a few times, I subconsciously register it in my head and how individual chords and tunes come together to produce a beautiful melody. My efforts finally bore fruit when, in Primary 5, I was awarded a hard-earned Distinction in the ABRSM Grade 8 Piano exam. As I commenced preparations for the PSLE, I began to play the piano more casually. Unlike the classical/baroque/romantic songs I was used to playing while formally learning piano, I was now playing songs I heard on the radio, old songs my father played for me on the car, my own melodies and so on. I also used my skill of improvisation to serve the community, by playing songs on request in nursing homes and childcare centres.

As I matured, my personal philosophy was to pursue only a few hobbies I enjoyed, but do whatever it takes to make each of them a skill. Being good wasn’t enough for me – I wanted to be the best at everything I did, rather than dabble in everything and only be adequate.

Some of the things that piqued my interest from young were math and fast typing. Driven to succeed, I pursued my interests like mad. I developed an active interest in computing and got my hands dirty, creating games, entering coding contests and hackathons, and initiating numerous computer science projects. One of my more memorable projects would be a machine learning project I did in the Raffles Science Institute and under the mentorship of Dr. Tan Guoxian. My team worked with speech recognition and machine learning algorithms to develop an indoor geolocation app using a Singapore-contextualised Automatic Speech Recognition engine; this was used to facilitate retail experiences of users and connect them to their items of interest. In my spare time, I also trained myself to become a proficient typist with an average speed of well over 150 words per minute, rivalling some of the best in the world!

I took this fiery drive of mine to Bridge, my co-curricular activity in JC. Heralded as the king of all card games, its competitive and calculative nature kept me hooked. I pored over bridge book after bridge book in the school library, determined to become the best player I could be. I found myself making trips to the Singapore Contract Bridge Association (SCBA) every Friday night to engage in social bridge games, and to hone my skill. Eventually, my efforts bore fruit – I represented Singapore at the Asia Pacific Bridge Federation Youth Team Championships held in Thailand in 2019, as the youngest member of the national under-20 team. We attained a commendable bronze medal for our efforts, losing out narrowly only to China and Japan.

I am tremendously grateful to my parents for shaping me into who I am today. Ultimately, I'm hoping to get a scholarship to study computer science, so that I can help contribute to the Singapore society at large and earn a stable income to support my family and loved ones. I hope you will enjoy viewing my portfolio and learning more about me! :-)
